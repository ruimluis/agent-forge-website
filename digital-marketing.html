Concerns

Common Questions from Agency Owners

Won't AI-generated content look generic and hurt our creative reputation?
AI doesn't replace your creative team — it amplifies them. Your designers still concept the hero creative. AI then produces the 47 variations (different sizes, platforms, headlines) that used to take days. Your creative vision, AI execution. Plus, AI handles the boring stuff (reports, scheduling, status updates) so creatives spend 80% of time on actual creative work.
Our clients expect a human touch
They still get it — from your strategists, not from scheduling tools and report generators. AI handles operational logistics invisibly. Your clients just notice they get faster responses, better reporting, and more strategic thinking from your team. They never know (or care) that AI scheduled their posts.
We already use tools like Hootsuite and Canva
Those tools help you work faster. Our AI actually does the work. Hootsuite requires a human to schedule each post. Our AI creates the content variations, writes the captions, optimizes posting times, schedules everything, and monitors performance — all autonomously. It's the difference between a tool and a team member.
What about brand voice and client-specific nuances?
We train the AI on each client's brand guidelines, past content, and approved voice. It learns what gets approved vs. rejected. For anything outside established patterns, it flags for human review. Think of it like a junior designer who's studied your client's brand book religiously.
This will eliminate jobs on my team
It eliminates tasks, not people. Your coordinators and junior designers move UP to more strategic work, or you serve 3x more clients with the same headcount. Most agencies use AI to scale revenue, not cut staff. The agencies that don't adopt AI? They're the ones who'll be cutting staff when they lose clients to faster competitors.
